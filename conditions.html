Understanding Your Condition

Chronic Joint Pain

Joint pain that persists for more than 3-6 months is considered chronic. It can result from arthritis, previous injuries, or degenerative changes. Our treatments focus on strengthening the supporting structures around joints to provide lasting relief.

Ligament & Tendon Injuries

Ligaments connect bones to bones, while tendons connect muscles to bones. Injuries to these structures often heal incompletely, leading to chronic pain and instability. Prolotherapy stimulates complete healing of these crucial tissues.

Spinal Conditions

Back and neck pain often stems from ligament laxity, disc problems, or facet joint issues. Our approach addresses the underlying structural problems rather than just masking symptoms with medication.

Sports-Related Injuries

Athletes require treatments that not only relieve pain but also restore function and prevent re-injury. Our regenerative therapies help athletes return to their sport stronger than before.

Arthritis Management

While arthritis cannot be "cured," its progression can be slowed and symptoms significantly reduced. Our treatments improve joint function and may delay or eliminate the need for surgery.

Overuse Syndromes

Repetitive stress injuries like tennis elbow or runner's knee respond well to regenerative treatments. We address both the immediate pain and the underlying weakness that led to the injury.

When to Seek Specialized Pain Management

Duration

Pain lasting more than 3 months despite conservative treatment

Impact

Pain that interferes with daily activities, work, or sleep

Failed Treatments

Previous treatments haven't provided lasting relief

Surgical Consideration

You're considering surgery and want to explore alternatives

Recurring Issues

Pain that keeps coming back after temporary improvement

Joint Instability

Joints that feel unstable, weak, or give out unexpectedly
