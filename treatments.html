Prolotherapy for Joint Pain

Joint pain can significantly impact your quality of life. Our targeted prolotherapy treatments help restore function and reduce pain in major joints.

Common Joint Conditions Treated:

  • Shoulder Pain: Rotator cuff injuries, frozen shoulder, labral tears, chronic instability
  • Knee Pain: Meniscus tears, ligament injuries, arthritis, patellar tendonitis
  • Hip Pain: Labral tears, bursitis, arthritis, hip flexor injuries
  • Ankle Pain: Chronic sprains, instability, arthritis, Achilles tendonitis
  • Elbow Pain: Tennis elbow, golfer's elbow, chronic tendonitis
  • Wrist & Hand: Carpal tunnel, arthritis, ligament injuries

Why Choose Prolotherapy for Joint Pain?

Unlike temporary pain relief methods, prolotherapy addresses the underlying weakness in ligaments and tendons that support your joints. This results in long-lasting improvement and reduced risk of re-injury.

Prolotherapy for Back & Neck Pain

Back and neck pain are among the most common reasons for seeking medical care. Our approach targets the ligaments and tendons that support your spine.

Conditions We Address:

  • Chronic lower back pain
  • Herniated or bulging discs
  • Sciatica and nerve pain
  • Cervical strain and whiplash injuries
  • Facet joint pain
  • Sacroiliac joint dysfunction
  • Thoracic spine pain
  • Failed back surgery syndrome

How Prolotherapy Helps:

By strengthening the ligaments that support your spine, prolotherapy can reduce instability and decrease the stress on spinal discs, often eliminating the need for surgery. The treatment promotes natural healing and tissue regeneration.

Arthritis Treatment

Arthritis doesn't have to limit your life. Our regenerative treatments can help reduce pain and improve joint function.

Types of Arthritis We Treat:

  • Osteoarthritis: The most common form, caused by wear and tear on joints
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Inflammatory arthritis requiring specialized management
  • Post-Traumatic Arthritis: Developing after injury to the joint
  • Degenerative Joint Disease: Progressive breakdown of joint cartilage

Treatment Approach:

We use a combination of prolotherapy, joint injections, and rehabilitation to reduce inflammation, strengthen supporting structures, and improve joint mechanics. Many patients experience significant relief and avoid or delay the need for joint replacement surgery.

Tendon Injury Treatment

Tendon injuries can be stubborn and slow to heal. Prolotherapy accelerates the healing process by stimulating new collagen formation.

Common Tendon Conditions:

  • Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis): Pain on the outside of the elbow
  • Golfer's Elbow (Medial Epicondylitis): Pain on the inside of the elbow
  • Rotator Cuff Tendonitis: Shoulder pain and weakness
  • Achilles Tendonitis: Pain and stiffness in the heel and ankle
  • Patellar Tendonitis: Knee pain, especially with jumping activities
  • De Quervain's Tenosynovitis: Wrist and thumb pain

Why Prolotherapy Works for Tendons:

Tendons have poor blood supply, which limits their natural healing ability. Prolotherapy increases blood flow to the area and stimulates the production of new, strong collagen fibers, resulting in lasting improvement.

Trigger Point Injections

Trigger points are tight, painful knots in muscles that can cause localized pain or refer pain to other areas. Trigger point injections provide rapid relief.

Conditions Treated:

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Tension headaches and migraines
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic muscle tension
  • Neck and shoulder pain
  • Lower back muscle spasms

What to Expect:

The procedure is quick, typically taking only a few minutes. Most patients experience immediate relief, with continued improvement over the following days. Multiple injections may be performed during a single visit to address all problem areas.
